About

Even Albert Einstein believed in the multiplicative impact of effort on raw talent. Angela Duckworth wrote a whole book on the subject of Grit, the stick-to-it-iveness that she says separates olympians from weekend joggers. Her formula for transforming raw potential into the best outcome is: 

talent * effort === skill
skill * effort === achievement

It is passion that drives that effort. When getting to the gym, picking up a cello, or, for me, sitting at a keyboard is most daunting, it is the love of the craft that persists. I love programming. I have always enjoyed building and engineering. I also have a long running obsession with technology that probably started with my NES. Software engineering is the collision of these two fields, and that is where I most want to spend my time and put my effort.
